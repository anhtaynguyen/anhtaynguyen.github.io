My research develops mechanics-based models and experimental interpretation tools for fracture and transport in geomaterials, with applications to hydraulic fracturing, geological carbon storage, geothermal energy, and subsurface energy systems.

## Regularized fracture modeling of quasi-brittle materials

I work on crack band and smooth crack band formulations for modeling damage localization, finite fracture process zones, and crack width evolution in quasi-brittle materials.

## Fast permeability testing of tight rocks

I develop rapid transient methods for estimating the permeability of low-porosity and low-permeability rocks using early-time pressure data, accounting for storage and system compliance.

## Multiphysics fracture and hydraulic fracture branching

I develop poromechanical models for hydraulic fracture propagation and branching in rocks with weak layers, including the effects of fluid flow, damage-dependent permeability, and evolving effective stress.

## Chemo-hydraulic and osmotic effects in shale

I study how osmotic pressure gradients and fluid-rock interactions can affect diffusion, effective stress, and long-term crack formation in shale.

## Related work: crack-parallel stress effects

I also study crack-parallel stress effects in soft and quasi-brittle materials through gap tests and microplane/crack-band modeling.
